What makes a compliant Issuer

- **Sec. 3(a)** — Only a "permitted payment stablecoin issuer" may legally issue a payment stablecoin in the US. Everything else in the Act defines what that status requires.
- **Sec. 2(23)** — Defines exactly who qualifies as a "permitted payment stablecoin issuer": a bank subsidiary, a federally qualified nonbank issuer approved under Sec. 5, or a state-qualified issuer approved by a state regulator.
- **Sec. 4(a)(1)(A)** — The core compliance requirement: reserves must back outstanding stablecoins at least 1:1, held only in specified safe assets (cash, Fed deposits, insured bank deposits, short-term Treasuries ≤93 days, and a few similarly liquid instruments).
- **Sec. 4(a)(1)(C)** — **This is your actual verification mechanism.** Issuers must publish, on their own website, a monthly report showing total outstanding stablecoins and the exact composition of reserves (amount, category, average maturity, geographic custody location).
- **Sec. 4(a)(3)(A)–(B)** — Each monthly reserve report must be examined by a registered public accounting firm, and personally certified as accurate by the issuer's CEO and CFO, with criminal penalties for a false certification.
- **Sec. 4(a)(10)(A)–(B)** — Issuers with more than $50 billion in outstanding stablecoins must additionally prepare a full annual audited financial statement (GAAP-compliant) and post it publicly, plus submit it to their regulator.
- **Sec. 5(a)–(d)** — The approval process itself: an issuer must apply to its primary regulator, which evaluates factors like financial condition, management integrity, and redemption policy before granting "permitted" status.
- **Sec. 5(i)** — Issuers must submit an annual certification to their regulator confirming their anti-money-laundering and sanctions programs are in place, this one goes to the regulator, not the public.
- **Sec. 8(b)(1)(A)** and **Sec. 18(c)(1)(F)/(b)(5)** — Public lists exist for cross-checking: the Treasury publishes noncompliant foreign issuers in the Federal Register, the Comptroller keeps a public list of approved foreign issuer registrations, and Treasury keeps a public list of foreign jurisdictions recognized as having comparable regulatory regimes.
- **Sec. 6(a)** — Ongoing supervision: regulators can request reports and conduct examinations of an issuer at any time to verify continued compliance, this is the regulator-side check that backs up the public disclosures.
